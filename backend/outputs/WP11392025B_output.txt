13. At the outset, it bears mentions that ‘Fertilizer’ is a
commodity listed as Item 2 of the Schedule to the Essential
Commodities Act and hence the provisions therein are squarely
applicable to matters pertaining to fertilizers.

@ 14. Firstly, the proceeding initiated against the Petitioners herein
are in violation of Sec 10 of the Essential Commodities Act which
mandates that ‘person in-charge of the company as well as
the company’ are to be arrayed as Accused in the event of any
contravention, In the present case, the 1* Petitioner is the
proprietor of the retail outlet named Coorg Agri Centre based in
Madikeri whereas the 2" Petitioner is a partner at the fertilizer
manufacturing partnership firm named Farmer Fertilizer
Company. Neither the retail proprietorship nor the manufacturing

Pl firm and its Responsible person have been arrayed as parties
thereby vitiating the entire prosecution. It is submitted that the
position of law regarding Sec 10 of EC Act is settled by the
decision of the Hon’ble Supreme Court in C.V. Parekh’s case
(1970 3 SCC 491) and thereafter by several decisions of this
Hon'ble court as well. Copy of the judgment in CRLP


--- Page 13 ---
7 {0

Court is produced herewith as Annexure-G.

15. There is utter non-application of mind by the learned Trial
court because cognizance of the alleged offence has been taken
without complying with Sec 202 of CrPC which mandates that if
the Accused resides outside the jurisdiction of the court, the

w Magistrate is required to conduct an ‘inquiry’ before issuance of
summons under Sec 204 of CrPC. The same is also in
contravention of the Hon’ble Supreme Courts’ decision in Vijay
Dhanuka case (2014 14 SCC 638). In the present case,
Petitioner No.2 is admittedly based out of Hassan and thus

compliance of Sec 202 CrPC becomes necessary.

16. The perusal of order sheet reveals non-application of mind as
the order taking cognizance is a non-descriptive and a non-

speaking order. In private complaint cases unlike the state
| P) prosecution cases there is a greater duty upon the Magistrate to
display application of mind through a reasoned order since the
facility of a full-scale investigation by the Police is not available
to the competent Magistrate, as per settled the law of precedent.

17. It is submitted that the 2™ Petitioner has been wrongly
arrayed as Accused No.2 although he is not the Responsible
person of the manufacturing firm, The License of the
